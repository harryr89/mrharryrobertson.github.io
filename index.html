MY WORK

Here are my projects from General Assembly


Project One

My first project was a game, a version of Wack-a-Mole. I found the the project fascinating because you have to think in many directions at once and at different places in time. This kind of parallel thinking was addictive because it showed me the beauty of intuitive connections and powerful abstractions. You can create a finer nuance in your thinking which makes you a better problem solver. My project was structured around a square grid with parts of the grid that would light up randomly and before they went out you had to click on them. If you failed to click them before their coloured state had vanished you would lose a life, you only have three lifes.

Project Two

I love landscapes and the expanse of nature,this gave me inspiration for my second project. I decided to make a website where people could upload their pictures. I made it specific to Scandinavia because it made it less general with some of the world's most spectacular scenery, Specifically I chose Norway. Using pictures as the main feature of an application gives you great opportunity to create a minimalist and balanced layout around the picture. I used bootstrap for the design and structure, this introduced me to the efficiency and futher enabled my understanding of using css frameworks.

Project Three

This was a group project. We made a text to speech app using the IBM computer Watson. We uploaded a text file to the application that was then pushed to Watson, converted by Watson from text to speech and sent back to our app so it could be played. This was quite a technical challenge we had to overcome because there were many variables to think about and different collective functionalities as well. I learnt so much from the rest of the group who all had great team spirit and where willing to help out each other,this opportunity helped to think of problems in different ways.

Project Four

Quality of conversation by qualification. I created a website which enabled people to create interest specific forums that could ensure quality of dicussion and then where other people could join that event with similar interests but with a twist. You had to qualify your interest to the event creator, this ensured that the quality of the event was high and that an outcome was far more likely to be achieved. For example if I was to create an event about 1980s jazz I would want to have an enriching discussion about this subject which means people having the same aims as me. This is achieved by people only being accepted to the event by demonstrating their knowledge of the subject to the group creator. To do this the online applicants would need to answer questions about the subject within a certain amount of time. The group creator could then accept or decline.
